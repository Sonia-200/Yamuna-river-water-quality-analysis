# 🌊Yamuna-river-water-quality-analysis using Power BI


## 📌 Project Overview
This project focuses on the **assessment of Yamuna River water quality** using a combination of **CPCB secondary data** and **manually collected primary samples**. The analysis was performed using **Power BI dashboard** to study temporal and spatial variations in water quality and identify major pollution trends.

The project aims to evaluate the impact of urban discharge and sewage contamination on river health and propose data-driven environmental insights.

---

## 🎯 Objectives
- Analyze Yamuna River water quality using CPCB historical data and manually collected samples.
- Study **temporal (year-wise)** and **spatial (location-wise)** variations in water quality.
- Identify pollution hotspots and major causes of water contamination.
- Create interactive dashboard for visual analysis and decision-making.

---

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies Used
- **Power BI** – Dashboard creation & data visualization  
- **Microsoft Excel / CSV** – Data preprocessing and organization  
- **CPCB Secondary Data** – Historical water quality records  
- **Primary Sampling Data** – Manually collected water samples

---

## 📊 Parameters Analyzed
The following water quality parameters were analyzed:

- **Temperature (°C)**
- **pH**
- **Dissolved Oxygen (DO)**
- **Biological Oxygen Demand (BOD)**
- **Nitrate (NO₃)**

---

## 📈 Dashboard Features
- **Temporal Analysis** – Year-wise changes in water quality parameters  
- **Spatial Analysis** – Location-wise comparison of pollution levels  
- **Trend Analysis** – Identification of pollution patterns over time  
- **Interactive Visualizations** – Easy interpretation of environmental data

---

## 🔍 Key Insights
### 🟢 Cleaner Zones
- **Palla and Wazirabad** showed relatively better water quality with higher dissolved oxygen and lower BOD levels.

### 🔴 Polluted Zones
- Water quality deteriorated significantly at **ITO, Nizamuddin, and ISBT Bridge**, indicating increased urban pollution impact.

### 🚨 Critical Pollution Zone
- **Okhla after meeting with Shahdara Drain** emerged as the most polluted location, showing:
  - Highest **BOD levels**
  - Lowest **Dissolved Oxygen**
  - Strong evidence of untreated sewage discharge

### 📉 Environmental Trend
- A strong **inverse relationship between BOD and Dissolved Oxygen** was observed, indicating worsening water quality with increasing organic pollution.

---

## 💡 Conclusion
The analysis highlights that **untreated sewage discharge and urban drain contamination are the major contributors to Yamuna River pollution**. While pH and nitrate levels remained within moderate ranges, **low dissolved oxygen and high BOD levels clearly indicate severe organic pollution in Delhi stretches of the river**.

The manually collected primary data aligned closely with historical CPCB trends, validating the reliability of the analysis.
